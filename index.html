The Mission

A Planet That Doesn't Want You Alive

You are Harper — a 23rd-century investigative journalist operating deep-cover with the Alterian Corps. One week ago, your partner Elliot vanished somewhere on the surface of Rigel V. No signal. No trace. No mercy.

Now you're on the ground, alone, in the pitch black of a Rigel V night. Sand shifts somewhere ahead. Your satchel hangs heavy at your hip. Somewhere in this ruined world lies a Doomsday Machine — and the answers you came for.

Find Elliot. Find the machine. Get out alive.

The Interface

Your Words Are Your Weapons

There are no buttons to mash, no health bars to watch. In Rigel's Revenge, you communicate directly with the world through natural language commands. Type what you want to do — and the world responds.

"Pitch black. The Rigel V night is absolute — you can't see your own hand. Sand shifts somewhere ahead, and you can feel rubble against your boots. Your satchel hangs heavy at your hip."

The writing is terse, atmospheric, and unforgiving — exactly as a 23rd-century field report should be. Every room description is a puzzle. Every item a potential clue.
